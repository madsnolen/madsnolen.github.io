What is a Database?

A database is an organzied collection of data that can be stored, accessed, and managed. Databases allow users and applications to store large amounts of information effeciently and retrieve it quickly when needed. Databases are commonly used in various applications, such as websites, mobile apps, and enterprise software, to manage and organize data effectively.

Why are Databases Important?

Databases are important because they provide a structured way to store and manage data, allowing for efficient retrieval and manipulation of information. Instead of storing data in spreadsheets or files, databases allow multiple users and systems to access and update information safely and concurrently. They enable organizations to make informed decisions, improve operational efficiency, and support various applications and services that rely on data.
